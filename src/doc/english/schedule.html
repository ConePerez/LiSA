The Schedule Window

This window allows to enter a sequence or a schedule as a matrix. Moreover, it offers more possibilities to manipulate an existing schedule than the main window.

How to get there:

Make shure a problem type and all problem parameter are defined. Choose Edit|Solution

General Options

Editing a schedule in this window needs some knowledge in scheduling theory. For more information see Latin Rectangles in Scheduling Theory - a Basic Modelling Concept.
  • The choice of Generate|Mark as semiactive forces the schedule to be semiactive. In this case, you cannot edit the schedule but only the sequence.
  • You may edit the sequence by entering any matrix. Choose Generate|Make feasible to transform your matrix into a feasible sequence. If your matrix was not a latin rectangle, LiSA will schedule jobs or machines with smaller index first.
  • You may generate a random sequence or reverse an existing one.
  • A double click on an operation will invoke the sequence editor.
After manipulation a sequence or schedule, you have to click the Adopt-button to make your changes take effect.

If this does not work:

  • If a schedule is forced to be semiactive you cannot edit the operation completion times.
  • Do not forget to click the Adopt-button to make your changes take effect.
