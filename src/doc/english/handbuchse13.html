4.2 Universal Constructive Procedures

Heuristic procedures are used for determining quickly an approximate solution. In contrast to exact procedures (→4.1), they deliver a solution in a substantially smaller running time. However, the quality of these solutions can be far away from an optimal solution.

LiSA contains in addition to simple constructive heuristics (dispatching rules) (→4.2.1), matching heuristics (→4.2.2) also beam search procedures for appending (→4.2.3) and inserting (→4.2.4) operations, respectively.
