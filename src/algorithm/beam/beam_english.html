The Beam Search Algorithm is a heuristical incomplete search technique. Hence it will not always dicover an optimal solution. The quality of the solution found by Beam-Search depends on the particular problem, as well as the several user settings.
 

procedure:

  • create an insertion order of the operations
  • for each of the operations :
    1. create new partial schedules by:
      • inserting the operation as first Job in job-order and machine-order
      • inserting the operation as immediate succsessor of an existing operation in the mashine-order
      • inserting the operation as immediate succsessor of an existing operation in the job-order
    2. choose the "best" of them and consider next operation
The choice of the "best" schedules is completely determined by the users settings of the Beamwidth and the Choice criterion.

settings:

  • Beamwidth:
      • Determines how many of the new created schedules are kept for further expansions.
      Note : Which of them is kept depends on the choice method/criterion.
  • Insertion order:
      • the order the operations are considered to be inserted :
      • LPT
      ordered by nondecreasing processing times
      • RANDOM
      randomly generated order
      • DIAGONAL1

        • Example:
           
          13 7 1 19 14 8
          20 15 9 2 21 16
          3 22 17 10 4 23
          11 5 24 18 12 6

         
      • DIAGONAL2

        • Similar to DIAGONAL1 but more straight forward:
           
          1 5 9 13 17 21
          22 2 6 10 14 18
          19 23 3 7 11 15
          16 20 24 4 8 12

           
      •  MACHINEWISE

        • column by column
  • Choice method:
      • Determines which schedules are kept
      • INSERT1

        • choose from the created new schedules according to the choice criterion
      • INSERT2

        • similar to INSERT1, but take only one new schedule for each base schedule
  • Choice criterion:
      • Determines the cost function of a partial schedule
      • CMAX

        • costs of the longest path in the schedule
      • CLAST

        • costs of the longest path through the last inserted operation

remarks:

As mentioned above the Beam-Search is a heuristic technique. Hence a partial schedule leading to an optimal solution could be thrown away. In this case we found other partial schedules with lower costs exhausting the beamwidth.
Note that the choice criterion CLAST does not yield monotonically non-decreasing costs, however it is more suitable with respect to the influence of the insertion of a particular operation, because with the CMAX criterion the last inserted operation has not to lie on the longest path in the partial schedule.
 

advices:

It is recommended to have several trials with different settings. In case you get an obviously unsatisfying solution you might have used an improper insertion order for that particular problem [ or the wrong algorithm :-)  ].
To increase probability of optimal results you should increase thebeamwidth. But note that very large values would lead to long running times.
Except CLAST the other settings do not have implicit effect on the running time ( CLAST uses Heads/Tails).
